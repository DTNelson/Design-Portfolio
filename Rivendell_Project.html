Rivendell Real Estate

Website Re-branding & Re-design

Rivendell Real Estate is a small, privately-owned real estate and property management company located in Denver, Colorado. The company's website needed a complete re-design and re-branding in a short timeframe. The goals of this project were to modernize the site while making it simple, clean, directive, and responsive to all devices. I also re-designed the company's logo, giving it a streamlined and cleaner look, while still maintaining its 'feel' as a real estate and property management company.

Original Site Assessment

Outdated UI/UX

Originally, the site was built with a bland Joomla template in a multi-page layout. Some pages appeared unfinished while others had no content organization. Sufficient content was available on the original site, but the site was not 100% responsive to smaller devices and had an outdated design. Due to a limited time frame, we wanted to salvage as much current content as possible while eliminating content that was not relevant or useful.

Gather Requirements

Needed vs. Nice-to-Haves

Over several meetings, Rivendell Real Estate's founding broker and I determined that the site needed several updates. Primarily, the site needed to be 100% responsive, a more modern look and feel, easily navigable, and a succinct call-to-action. To simplify information-sharing and gathering, we all collaborated and shared ideas through Google Docs.


How Can We Stand Out?

Rivendell's three brokers had such favorable feedback from clients over the years, they wanted to showcase these testimonials. The brokers felt that most real estate or property management companies focus solely on the properties, not on the client experience, so they wanted to bring their clients to the forefront.

Site Map

Simple Navigation

After deciding on a one-page website format, we wanted every page easily accessible from the Home, or landing page. The resulting site map was straightforward: The Home/Landing Page + Call to Action, Our Services, About Us, Testimonials, MLS Listings, Contact Us, and links to the Property Management Login Portal.



Brand Identity

New Logo

Another goal was to redesign the company's logo while maintaining the look and feel of a real estate and property management company. The brokers' goal was to design a simple yet modern logo, so that the colors can be interchanged easily to accommodate open house signs, business cards, light vs dark backgrounds, etc.

Call-to-Action Photo

I wanted Rivendell's call to action to be prominent, with a visually strong image or photo that flowed easily with their new logo and company name. I spent several hours scouring Unsplash, Stocksy, Fololia, Bigstock, Death to the Stock Photo, you name it.

Ultimately, we decided on a large lock and key photo, which provided depth, perspective and a feeling that you can enter the home, whether you own or lease. Countless real estate companies focus on luxury properties, modern architecture, modern design, etc., devoid of a personal touch. The website's main call-to-action button is a bright, inviting coral color, and the main copywriting compels you to find out more information about the company and brokers.

Wireframes

One Page Responsive Web Design

I researched several Bootstrap and landing page designs and layouts on several sites, including Wrapbootstrap, Land-Book, and Onepagelove to get some ideas for the site's general organization. Using Balsamiq for Google Drive, I mocked up wireframes for Rivendell's site, sharing the low-fidelity wireframes with the group.

UI Design

High Fidelity Mockups

Although I use PhotoShop and Illustrator hand-in-hand, my mockup tool of choice is Illustrator - for me, it's much quicker to grab layers, select elements, and edit text. As I created the layout, the carousel design morphed into a single-photo, single-description layout so the user can focus on one property and testimonial at a time. I shared my prototypes via Invision with my design mentor at Bloc.io, the Rivendell brokers, and several design professionals.

Coding the Site

Bootstrap Framework

Upon recommendation from my Bloc.io mentor at the beginning of the project, I decided to use the Bootstrap framework since it has extensive and thorough documentation and resources for responsive HTML/CSS development. To further understand the capabilities of Bootstrap, I also viewed several Lynda.com Bootstrap tutorials from Jen Kramer and Ray Villalobos.

  • A Bootstrap Carousel highlights Rivendell's client experiences and homes purchased and leased through the brokers.
  • The brokers' casual photos help add to their approachability.
  • The contact form was also required - the brokers needed a way they could easily refer potential and current clients to submit questions, comments, and concerns.
