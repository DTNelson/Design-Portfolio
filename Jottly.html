Jott.ly Landing Page

Web Site Design & Development

My first assignment at Bloc.io involved designing a landing page for a ficticious SAAS content sharing site called Jott.ly. My mentor, who served as client and end-user, provided basic requirements for the site. The site needed to convey a simple and short call-to-action message, provide basic product/pricing information, and be responsive to all devices.

Logo Design

We were tasked with logo design and creation in Illustrator, which, in my opinion, was the most difficult task in the assignment. I searched for inspiration from common sites including Dribble, Land-Book, Logopond, Behance, and others. I didn’t want to reproduce several previously completed Jott.ly logo themes containing pen and paper. I opted to create an interconnected circle that conveyed a feeling of cohesiveness.

Color Palette

Kuler, Coolors, or Color?

I was torn between two general color schemes. My first theme, Coral Midnight, consisted of complementary colors: coral call-to-action buttons with a midnight blue overlay and off-white secondary colors. The second theme, Rainy Seafoam, contained a bright teal call-to-action color surrounded by neutral grays.


Typography

Raleway & Lato

For Jott.ly's headers, I opted for the Google's Raleway, an ornate and modern sans serif typeface. Since it is so light and thin, I used Raleway only for headers and navigation menus. I selected Google’s Lato for the body typeface - it’s readable, friendly, and classy, less 'boring' than the more commonly-used typefaces such as Open Sans, Arial, or Helvetica.

Wireframes

Two Design Options

I used a free version of Balsamiq for Google Drive to create and share wireframes of the site layout. My general layout showcased a large, powerful image at the top. I crafted two versions of the landing page’s remaining sections:

  1. In the first wireframe, prominent CTA text and a large CTA button display near the photo’s center, in the middle of the circle of friends. Jott.ly’s primary features follow in the second section, and the available pricing plans are listed in the third.
  2. In the second wireframe, prominent CTA text is placed to left of the main photo, so it is not obscured. I added three goals Jott.ly users could accomplish in the second section, and added client testimonials in the last.

Mockups

Two Options

I used grids from the 960.gs site for guidance and mocked up two versions of the landing page - the first in PhotoShop and second in Illustrator.

The first mockup has a midnight blue and coral color palette, with the main image showcasing a circle of friends; The second mockup contains teal call-to-action buttons with a neutral gray color palette overlaying a vintage typewriter near pen and paper. I quickly learned my mockup tool of choice is Illustrator - for me, it's much easier to find and grab layers, select elements, and most importantly, edit text.

Coding the Site

From Scratch

Upon recommendation from my Bloc.io mentor at the beginning of the project, I decided to create my own general grid, since neither of my designs really fit a framework or boilerplate. In reality, developing the site from scratch enabled me to learn about CSS3/HTML5 and responsive web design through research, trial, and many errors.
